## What is Muse Video?

**Muse Video** is Meta's forthcoming video-generation model, previewed in July 2026. It shares Muse Image's pretraining base and is designed to generate video with native audio while following detailed prompts and maintaining visual and temporal consistency. Meta says it is coming soon to creators and Meta AI; it is not presented as a generally available public API. Meta also openly notes remaining gaps in audio-video synchronization and physically accurate fast motion.

The cases below treat each prompt as a production brief: visible action, camera behavior, timing, sound, continuity constraints, and the proof video stay together.

- **Start from text or a frame** - Generate from a written scene or animate an image that already carries the composition
- **Direct observable motion** - Describe blocking, momentum, object interaction, and the physical consequence of each action
- **Write beats, not a synopsis** - Use timestamps or a short sequence of actions when timing and reveals matter
- **Generate sound with the scene** - Include dialogue, ambience, music, or effects when audio is part of the storytelling
- **Lock exact counts and identities** - State which subjects, faces, objects, and spatial relationships must remain stable
- **Protect continuity explicitly** - State which face, product geometry, layout, wardrobe, or background must not drift

**Current references:** [Meta AI engineering overview](https://ai.meta.com/blog/introducing-muse-image-muse-video-msl/) · [Meta product announcement](https://about.fb.com/news/2026/07/introducing-muse-image-meta-ai/) · [Create videos on ImagineVid](https://imaginevid.io/reference-video)

### Turn a Prompt into a Shot Template

Reusable video prompts separate scene variables from directing logic. Replace the subject, setting, spoken line, or product while preserving the tested camera path, beat structure, sound plan, and continuity rules.

**Template pattern:**
```
[DURATION + ASPECT RATIO]. [SUBJECT] performs [VISIBLE ACTION] in [SETTING]. Camera: [FRAMING + MOVE]. Beats: [TIMED ACTIONS]. Audio: [DIALOGUE + FOLEY + AMBIENCE]. Preserve: [IDENTITY / PRODUCT / LAYOUT]. Avoid: [FAILURE MODES].
```

Begin with one action and one camera idea. Add timing, audio, and preservation constraints only when they solve a visible production need; then change one variable at a time between generations.

An editorial reconstruction of Meta's official preview clip, designed to test scale, occlusion, reactions, and room continuity in one comic shot.

#### Source prompt

```
A photorealistic elephant stands improbably inside a small, warmly lit dining room during a formal dinner party. Six adults sit around a table with roast dinner, candles, flowers, and wine glasses. The elephant fills the back of the room without breaking the walls or furniture; its trunk reaches forward as the guests notice it and react with restrained surprise, then laughter. One continuous eye-level shot with subtle handheld drift, natural eyelines, believable contact shadows, consistent elephant scale, warm practical lamps, room tone, quiet cutlery, chair movement, and a soft elephant breath. No cuts, no duplicated guests, no changing table layout.
```

#### #1: Exactly Five Ducklings Climb a Step

![Language-EN](https://img.shields.io/badge/Language-EN-blue)

##### Why the workflow matters

A strict continuity prompt that keeps five animals stable while one delayed action creates a beginning, obstacle, and resolution.

##### Source prompt

```
A mother duck leads a line of exactly FIVE ducklings toward a curb; the fifth and smallest duckling struggles at the step, then hops up to rejoin the others. There are exactly five ducklings in total — the same five throughout the entire video. Never add, remove, duplicate, or change the number of ducklings; keep all five visible and consistent from start to finish. Cozy indoor lamplight, authentic home-video look, slightly imperfect handheld framing with natural shake. One continuous approximately 10-second moment with a clear beginning, obstacle, and payoff. Audio: soft webbed footsteps, tiny chirps, room tone, a small scrape at the curb, then a bright chirp when the last duckling succeeds.
```
